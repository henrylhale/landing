How I Work

Every question has four wrong answers, and every wrong answer is there for a reason. We'll look through your practice tests and learn to identify the flaw each wrong option exhibits. There are only so many types of flaw: we'll figure out which ones are most persistent for you, give them memorable names, and practice recognizing them until they don't trip you up any more. A key part of this strategy is learning to write questions. You can really prove that you understand how to recognize wrong answers if you can cook them up yourself!

Who am I?

I have worked as an instructor at MathILy-Er, a selective summer math program for high schoolers, and as a software engineer at Amazon. I scored a 180 on the LSAT, but decided not to pursue a legal career. Before that, I earned degrees in math and computer science from the University of Chicago. My math background helps me teach you to strip away distractions and focus on the mechanics of the argument. Much like debugging code or fixing a proof, I teach you to isolate the error so you never make the same mistake twice.
