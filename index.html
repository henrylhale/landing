Pedagogy

The LSAT is not a test of memorization, but of formal logic and reading precision. My approach treats the exam with the rigour of a mathematical proof. Drawing from my background in theoretical mathematics and software engineering, I teach students to deconstruct arguments into their constituent axioms and logical flows. We do not learn "tricks"; we learn the language of the test itself.

Previously, I taught advanced mathematics to high-potential high school students at a selective summer program, where I refined my ability to distill complex abstract concepts into actionable understanding.
